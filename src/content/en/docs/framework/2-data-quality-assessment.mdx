# Numerator Assessment

High-quality health facility data form the foundation of reliable coverage estimates. This module systematically evaluates your data for completeness, accuracy, and internal consistency—identifying potential issues before they distort coverage calculations. By assessing reporting rates, detecting outliers, and verifying logical relationships between indicators, the Data Suite ensures your analysis builds on robust evidence.

The data quality assessment uses standardized metrics across a six-year period (2019–2024) to track progress and identify systemic problems. Key checks include comparing reported ratios of linked indicators (e.g., ANC1 to Penta1), identifying facilities with implausibly high or low values, and calculating an overall data quality score. This rigorous screening prevents garbage-in-garbage-out analyses while highlighting which districts or years require additional scrutiny or exclusion.

#### Key Considerations for Ratio Calculations

##### 1. ANC1 to Penta1 Ratio

Baseline assumptions:
- Pregnancy loss: **3%**  
- Stillbirth rate: **2%**  
- Twinning rate: **+1.5%**  
- Neonatal mortality: **3%**

Baseline expected ratio:

$$Expected\ Ratio = 1.07$$

Adjusted using survey coverage:

$$Expected\ Ratio = 1.07 \times \left(\frac{ANC1\ Coverage}{Penta1\ Coverage}\right)$$

**Examples:**
- Equal coverage → **1.07**  
- 90% vs 95% → **1.01**

<Callout type="warning">
Acceptable range: **±0.05** from expected value.
</Callout>

---

##### 2. Penta1 to Penta3 Ratio

Driven by immunization drop-out rates:

Example:

$$\frac{0.95}{0.85} = 1.12$$

<Callout type="warning">
Acceptable range: **±0.05**
</Callout>
