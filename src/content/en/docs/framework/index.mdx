# Framework Overview

This page summarizes the CD2030 DataSuite analytical framework using the structure described in `Data Suite Guidebook_VaxxApp (1).docx`. It is intended to help teams move from raw facility and survey data to defensible national and sub-national coverage findings.

## End-to-end workflow

The framework follows a practical sequence:

1. Load and configure facility and survey data.
2. Calibrate key national benchmark rates.
3. Assess data quality before estimating coverage.
4. Adjust incomplete, unstable, or unreliable data.
5. Select the best-performing denominator.
6. Interpret national, equity, and sub-national results.
7. Use findings for planning, review, and action.

See the [workflow page](/en/docs/framework/workflow) for the process view.

## Key ideas from the guidebook

### Data inputs and setup

The guidebook begins with loading routine health facility data, restoring saved work where needed, and connecting external survey sources. It also highlights the importance of entering calibration inputs such as ANC1 coverage, Penta1 coverage, pregnancy loss, twinning, stillbirth rate, and mortality assumptions so the analysis is aligned with recent survey evidence.

### Data quality comes first

Before coverage is interpreted, the guidebook recommends checking whether the data is fit for use. This includes reporting completeness, extreme outlier detection, missing data patterns, and internal consistency checks such as comparing ANC1 and Penta1 ratios.

### Adjustment should be explicit

Where reporting is incomplete or unstable, analysts may exclude unreliable years, apply service-specific k-factors, and impute values for missing months or corrected outliers. The emphasis is on making those decisions transparent and reviewing how much they change the resulting indicators.

### Denominator choice drives credibility

The framework treats denominator assessment as a core analytical step, not a technical detail. Administrative projections, UN estimates, and survey-calibrated ANC1 or Penta1 denominators should be compared, and the preferred method should be the one that best aligns with recent survey results.

### National averages are not enough

The guidebook places strong emphasis on both inequality and geography. National findings should be interpreted alongside equity analyses across wealth, education, and residence, as well as sub-national comparisons across regions and districts.

### Analysis should support decisions

The final purpose of the framework is practical use. Findings should inform country review processes, planning discussions, and technical working groups so that the analysis leads to concrete RMNCAH action rather than standing alone as a report.

## Expected outputs

Teams using this framework should be able to produce:

- A documented data quality assessment and summary scorecard.
- A cleaned and adjusted facility dataset ready for analysis.
- A justified denominator choice for coverage estimation.
- National coverage trends compared with survey benchmarks.
- Equity and sub-national findings that identify who and where is being left behind.
- Evidence that can be used for planning, prioritization, and communication.
