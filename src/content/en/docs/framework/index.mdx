# Framework Overview

This page summarizes the CD2030 DataSuite analytical framework.
It is intended to help teams move from raw facility and survey data to defensible national and sub-national coverage findings.

## End-to-end workflow

The framework follows a practical sequence:

1. [Load and configure](/docs/framework/1-analysis-setup) facility and survey data.
2. [Calibrate key national benchmark rates](/docs/framework/1-analysis-setup/#survey-params).
3. [Assess data quality](/docs/framework/2-data-quality-assessment) before estimating coverage.
4. [Adjust](/docs/framework/3-data-adjustment) incomplete, unstable, or unreliable data.
5. [Select the best-performing denominator](/docs/framework/4-denominator-selection).
6. Generate and interprete **[coverage](/docs/framework/5-coverage-estimation)** , **[equity](/docs/framework/6-equity-analysis)**, **[sub-national](/docs/framework/7-subnational-analysis)**, **[mortality](/docs/framework/mortality)**, **[Service utilisation](/docs/framework/10-service-utilisation)** and **[Health system performance](/docs/framework/11-health-system-performance)** results.

## Analytical Methods

### Step 1: Data Quality Assessment

**Scientific Foundation:** Routine facility data are an indispensable source for population-level health indicators, but quality varies widely due to reporting inconsistencies, data entry errors, and systemic gaps. 
Standardized assessment enables tracking of progress over time and prevents unreliable data from contaminating coverage estimates.

**Approach:** The Data Suite evaluates monthly district-level data across a six-year period (2019–2024) using five core quality metrics:

- **Reporting Completeness** – Percentage of expected facility reports actually received (&ge;90% is considered acceptable). Calculated as the mean across four reporting forms (ANC, delivery, immunization, OPD).
2. **Extreme Outliers** – Monthly values exceeding 5 times the median absolute deviation (MAD), flagged for investigation. Data entry errors (e.g., accidental zeros) are common culprits.
3. **Internal Consistency** – Logical ratios between linked indicators are verified:
   - ANC1 to Penta1 ratio expected ~1.07 (accounting for pregnancy loss, stillbirth, twinning, neonatal mortality)
   - Penta1 to Penta3 ratio varies by dropout rates but should be stable
4. **Data Completeness** – Missing values within active reporting periods are identified.
5. **Overall Data Quality Score** – Computed automatically as the mean of key metrics to enable comparison across years and districts.

**DataSuite Implementation:** Navigate to the Data Quality Assessment module, select your indicator and analysis level (region or district), configure performance thresholds (default 90% reporting rate), and run the analysis. The platform generates interactive dashboards identifying problem districts, visualizes outlier distributions, tracks ratio trends, and downloads summary reports.

**Examples:** If a district drops to 60% reporting in 2022 due to system outage, that year is flagged unreliable. If ANC1 reports suddenly exceed Penta1 by &gt;50%, further investigation reveals either genuine access gaps or reporting anomalies requiring resolution.

### Step 3: Denominator Selection

**Scientific Foundation:** Coverage ratios require both accurate numerators (from facility reports) and accurate denominators (population estimates). Population projections are often unreliable, especially at district level. The Data Suite compares five denominator options by testing which produces coverage estimates most closely matching gold-standard household surveys (DHS, MICS).

**Five Denominator Methods:**

1. **DHIS2 Population Projections** – Built-in system estimates; varies widely in quality
2. **UN Population Projections** – International standard; available only at national level
3. **ANC1-Derived Target** – Back-calculates pregnant women from high-utilization facility visits: $\text{Pregnancies} = \frac{\text{Facility ANC1 visits}}{\text{Survey ANC1 coverage}}$
4. **Penta1-Derived Target** – Back-calculates eligible infants similarly
5. **Penta1 Adjusted for Growth** – Refines Penta1 estimates by applying annual growth rates

**Demographic Cascade Logic:** Maternal and child denominators are linked through standard demographic assumptions (applied globally, customizable by country):

- Pregnancy loss: 3%
- Stillbirth rate: 2%
- Twinning rate: 1.5%
- Neonatal mortality: 3%
- Postneonatal mortality: 2.4%

Starting with ANC1 facility visits and survey coverage, the cascade flows: pregnancies → deliveries (after loss) → live births (after stillbirth, twinning adjustments) → eligible infants (after neonatal mortality).

**Denominator Selection Rule:** Compare adjusted facility-based coverage for two tracer indicators (institutional deliveries, Penta3) across each denominator option against recent survey benchmarks. The method minimizing absolute coverage gaps is selected as the active denominator for all subsequent analyses.

**DataSuite Implementation:** Load UN projections, DHIS2 data, and recent survey data. The module automatically computes all five denominators, calculates coverage estimates using each, and visualizes side-by-side comparison with survey confidence intervals. Results highlight the winning denominator.

**Examples:** If Penta1-derived denominators produce facility coverage 85% ± 2% vs. survey 86% ± 4%, while DHIS2-derived produce 92% ± 5%, Penta1-derived is selected as superior.

### Step 4: Coverage Estimation

**Scientific Foundation:** Coverage—the proportion of the population receiving a needed service—is the primary metric for tracking progress toward universal health coverage. Estimation requires validated numerators (from quality-assessed and adjusted facility data) and evidence-based denominators (selected via comparison with surveys). Coverage is compared with global targets (e.g., 90% institutional delivery by 2025) and with survey estimates to assess credibility.

**Coverage Calculation:**
$$\text{Coverage (\%)} = \frac{\text{Numerator (Adjusted)}}{\text{Denominator (Selected)}} \times 100$$

**Key Handling Rules:**

- **Implausible Coverage >100%:** Indicates data quality issues or duplicate recording. Reframe as average services per capita (e.g., 1.2 doses per child rather than 120% coverage).
- **Family Planning:** Uses specialized FPET demographic model integrating multiple survey data sources; facility data included if quality permits.
- **Immunization:** Denominators account for child survival: Eligible infants = Live births − Neonatal deaths (− Postneonatal deaths for Measles1).

**Facility vs. Survey Concordance:** Coverage trends from facility data are plotted alongside survey estimates (with confidence intervals) to visually assess agreement. Large gaps signal systematic bias in numerators, denominators, or both, warranting investigation.

**DataSuite Implementation:** Navigate to Coverage Estimation, select your indicators, confirm denominator choice, and generate annual trend lines (2019–2024) alongside survey comparison plots. Module exports annual estimates, confidence intervals, and concordance assessments.

**Examples:** Institutional delivery facility-based: 82% (2024). Recent survey: 85% ± 3%. Gap of 3 percentage points within confidence interval—good concordance indicates reliable estimates.

### Step 5: Equity Analysis

**Scientific Foundation:** National coverage averages mask disparities that determine whether health systems serve all populations equitably. Three dimensions of inequality are critical: residence (urban/rural), wealth (richest-to-poorest quintiles), and maternal education. Inequalities reveal where health systems fail and guide targeted interventions.

**Inequality Patterns:**

1. **Top Inequality** – Only rich escape; poorest left far behind. Signals mass deprivation requiring universal access campaigns.
2. **Linear Inequality** – Wealth-associated gradients; each quintile shows incremental improvement. Suggests pro-poor targeting is working partially.
3. **Bottom Inequality** – Richest far ahead; middle/poor bunched together. Signals marginalization of the poorest requiring specific outreach.

**Approach:** Equiplots visualize coverage trajectories by population subgroup alongside national trend, enabling visual assessment of:

- Current inequality level (gap between richest and poorest)
- Trend direction (narrowing or widening over time)
- Progress toward universal coverage (all subgroups ≥target threshold)

**DataSuite Implementation:** In Equity Assessment (Equiplots), select your indicator and inequality dimension (residence, wealth, education). Module generates interactive plots stratified by survey year, allowing tracking over 6–10-year periods. Download summary tables showing coverage by quintile/subgroup and percentage meeting targets.

**Interpretation Guidance:** Answer: (1) Is current inequality large or small? (2) Is it improving? (3) What pattern emerges and what does it signal about program priorities?

### Step 6: Sub-National Analysis

**Scientific Foundation:** Geographic variation in coverage reveals health system inequities and allocative inefficiencies. National averages of 80% may hide one region at 95% and another at 60%—a critical actionability gap. Sub-national analysis identifies underperforming administrative units for targeted investment and monitors whether facility system strengthening reduces geographic disparities.

**Analytical Levels:**

- **Regional (Admin1):** Larger jurisdictions with more stable estimates; reference level for targeting districts
- **District (Admin2):** Smaller units with more volatility due to small populations and referral patterns (municipal hospitals serve rural hinterlands)

**Key Outputs:**

1. **District One-Pagers** – Visual summaries per region pairing:
   - Facility-based trend lines (2019–2024)
   - Recent survey snapshot with confidence interval
   - Bar graph comparing regional performance vs. all other regions
   - District-level table with 2024 coverage for institutional deliveries and Penta3

2. **Composite Coverage Index** – Average of seven RMNCAH indicators (ANC4, institutional delivery, SBA, IPT2, postnatal care, Penta3, Measles1) mapped geographically to identify strongest/weakest regions

3. **Sub-National Inequality Index** – Median Absolute Deviation from Median (MADM) quantifies spread in coverage among districts; tracks whether geographic disparities are narrowing

**DataSuite Implementation:** Navigate to Subnational Analysis, select admin level (region or district), choose indicator or use Custom Check for any metric. Module generates trend visualizations, downloadable one-pagers, and inequality summaries. Map interface shows composite coverage index by geography.

**Examples:** District A: Institutional delivery 88% (2024) with 3-year upward trend. District B (neighboring): 62%, static trend. District B is priority for facility strengthening investment.

---

### Step 7: Interpretation & Action

**Scientific Foundation:** Analysis is only valuable if findings drive decision-making. Interpretation bridges technical rigor and actionability by synthesizing findings, highlighting key messages, and explicitly linking results to program/policy options.

**Interpretation Framework:**

1. **Data Quality Verdict** – Are findings credible? Are outlier years or problem districts invalidating estimates?
2. **Coverage Status vs. Targets** – How close to national/global targets? On track or lagging?
3. **Trends** – Improving, stagnating, or declining? Pace of change?
4. **Equity Picture** – Which populations left behind? Patterns suggest: mass access gap, pro-poor targeting needed, or marginalization of poorest?
5. **Geographic Focus** – Which regions/districts most need investment?
6. **Anomalies** – Facility vs. survey discordance, unexpected ratios, or outlier districts require explanation (e.g., referral hub, recent campaign, local conflict).
7. **Recommendations** – For each finding, propose specific action: policy change, facility strengthening, equipment investment, training, campaign, etc.

**DataSuite Reports:** The platform generates standardized summary tables and visualizations ready for presentation to Ministry officials, donor partners, and technical working groups. Customize messaging for each audience while maintaining analytical integrity.
